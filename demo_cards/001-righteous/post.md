## Word
**RIGHTEOUS** — Acting in accord with divine or moral law; free from guilt or sin.

## Old Testament
> Habakkuk 2:4 — “...but the righteous shall live by his faith.”

## New Testament
> Romans 1:17 — “...as it is written, 'The righteous shall live by faith.'”

## Trivia
- In both Hebrew and Greek, the words for 'righteousness' and 'justice' share the same linguistic root.
- Noah is the first person explicitly called 'righteous' in the Bible (Genesis 6:9).
- The phrase 'The righteous shall live by faith' is quoted three times in the New Testament: Romans, Galatians, and Hebrews.
- Jesus is referred to as 'the Righteous One' in Acts 7:52 and 1 John 2:1.
