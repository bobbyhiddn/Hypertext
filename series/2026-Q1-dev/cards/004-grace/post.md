## Word
**GRACE** — Unmerited favor; a gift freely given without claim or expectation of return.

## Old Testament
> Genesis 6:8 — “But Noah found grace in the eyes of the LORD.”

## New Testament
> Ephesians 2:8 — “For by grace you have been saved through faith.”

## Trivia
- The Greek word 'charis' is the etymological root of the English words 'charity' and 'charisma'.
- In the Old Testament, 'chen' often implies a superior stooping down to show kindness to an inferior.
- The Apostle Paul uses the word 'grace' over 100 times across his epistles.
- Grace is often distinguished from mercy: mercy is not receiving the punishment one deserves, while grace is receiving a blessing one does not deserve.
