## Word
**SEE** — To perceive with the eyes; to discern, experience, or understand spiritually.

## Old Testament
> Psalm 34:8 — "Taste and see that the LORD is good; blessed is the man who takes refuge in him!"

## New Testament
> Matthew 5:8 — "Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God."

## Trivia
- The Hebrew root 'ra'ah' forms the word 'ro'eh' (seer), an early title for prophets in Israel.
- In biblical thought, 'seeing' often denotes deep understanding or experience rather than just visual perception.
- Hagar names God 'El Roi' (The God Who Sees Me) in Genesis 16, the only occurrence of this title.
- Jesus' invitation 'Come and you will see' (John 1:39) emphasizes personal verification over hearsay.
