## Word
**GRACE** — Unmerited favor; a free gift.

## Old Testament
> Genesis 6:8 — "But Noah found grace in the eyes of the LORD."

## New Testament
> Ephesians 2:8 — "For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves..."

## Trivia
- "Charis" (Greek) originally meant "beauty" or "charm" before the New Testament gave it deep theological weight.
- "Chen" (Hebrew) comes from a root meaning "to bend or stoop," depicting a superior showing kindness to an inferior.
- Grace is often defined as receiving what you do not deserve, while mercy is not receiving the punishment you do deserve.
- The Apostle Paul uses the word "grace" over 100 times in his letters, making it a central theme of his theology.
