## Word
**MAGI** — Wise men, astrologers, or priests from the East who visited the infant Jesus.

## Old Testament
> Jeremiah 39:3 — “...Nergal-Sharezer the Rab-mag, and all the rest of the officials...”

## New Testament
> Matthew 2:1 — “...Magi from the east came to Jerusalem...”

## Trivia
- Derived from the Old Persian word 'magu', referring to a priestly caste skilled in astrology.
- Scripture never states there were three men, only three gifts: gold, frankincense, and myrrh.
- The title 'Rab-mag' (Chief Magus) appears in Jeremiah, a rank Daniel likely held in Babylon.
- They were not kings, but likely advisors and astronomers from the Parthian Empire.
