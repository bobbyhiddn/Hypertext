## Word
**MAGI** — A priestly caste of Persian astrologers and dream interpreters; the 'wise men' who sought the Messiah.

## Old Testament
> Jeremiah 39:3 — “Then all the princes of the king of Babylon came in... Nergal-sharezer the Rab-mag...”

## New Testament
> Matthew 2:1 — “Now after Jesus was born in Bethlehem... behold, magi from the east came to Jerusalem.”

## Trivia
- The term originally referred to a priestly caste in the Persian Empire, likely Zoroastrian.
- Scripture never states there were three men, only that they brought three types of gifts.
- In the book of Daniel, the prophet was made chief over the 'wise men' of Babylon.
- The Greek word 'magos' is also the root for the English word 'magic' and 'magician'.
