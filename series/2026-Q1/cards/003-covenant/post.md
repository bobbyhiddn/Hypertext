## Word
**COVENANT** — A solemn, binding agreement or compact between two parties, often sealed by an oath or blood sacrifice.

## Old Testament
> Jeremiah 31:31 — “Behold, the days are coming... when I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel.”

## New Testament
> Luke 22:20 — “This cup that is poured out for you is the new covenant in my blood.”

## Trivia
- The Hebrew phrase 'karat berit' literally means 'to cut a covenant,' referring to the ritual of cutting animals in two.
- There are five major covenants in the Old Testament: Noahic, Abrahamic, Mosaic, Davidic, and the New Covenant prophesied by Jeremiah.
- The Greek word 'diatheke' was chosen by Septuagint translators over 'syntheke' to emphasize God's initiative rather than a mutual contract.
- Covenants are typically accompanied by a sign, such as the rainbow, circumcision, or the Sabbath.
