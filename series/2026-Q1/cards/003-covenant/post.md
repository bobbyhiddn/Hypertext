## Word
**COVENANT** — A solemn, binding agreement between two parties, often sealed by an oath or blood sacrifice.

## Old Testament
> Jeremiah 31:33 — “I will put my law in their minds and write it on their hearts. I will be their God, and they will be my people.”

## New Testament
> Luke 22:20 — “This cup is the new covenant in my blood, which is poured out for you.”

## Trivia
- The Hebrew word 'berit' comes from a root meaning 'to cut,' referring to the ancient custom of walking between cut animal pieces to seal a pact.
- In Greek, 'diatheke' can also translate to 'last will and testament,' implying an inheritance.
- Jeremiah 31 is the only place in the Old Testament where the phrase 'New Covenant' is explicitly used.
- Major biblical covenants include those with Noah, Abraham, Moses, and David.
