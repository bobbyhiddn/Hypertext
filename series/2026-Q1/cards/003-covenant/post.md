## Word
**COVENANT** — A solemn, binding agreement between two parties, often sealed by an oath or blood sacrifice.

## Old Testament
> Jeremiah 31:31 — “I will make a new covenant with the house of Israel and with the house of Judah.”

## New Testament
> Luke 22:20 — “This cup that is poured out for you is the new covenant in my blood.”

## Trivia
- The Hebrew idiom 'karat berith' literally means 'to cut a covenant', referring to the practice of cutting sacrificial animals.
- The Greek term 'diatheke' was chosen over 'syntheke' to emphasize God's sovereign initiative rather than a mutual contract.
- Major biblical covenants include the Noahic, Abrahamic, Mosaic, Davidic, and the New Covenant.
- In the ancient Near East, parties would walk between cut animal pieces to invoke a curse upon themselves if they broke the vow.
