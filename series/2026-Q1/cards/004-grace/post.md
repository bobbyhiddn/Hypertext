## Word
**GRACE** — Unmerited favor; a gift freely given.

## Old Testament
> Genesis 6:8 — “But Noah found grace in the eyes of the LORD.”

## New Testament
> Ephesians 2:8 — “For by grace you have been saved through faith...”

## Trivia
- The Greek word *charis* is the root of English words like 'charity' and 'charisma'.
- In the Old Testament, *chen* comes from a root meaning 'to bend or stoop' in kindness.
- Grace is often distinguished from mercy: mercy is withholding punishment, while grace is bestowing blessing.
- The Apostle Paul uses this term over 100 times to describe God's unearned favor.
