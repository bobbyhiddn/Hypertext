## Word
**GOSPEL** — Good news; a joyful announcement of victory or salvation

## Old Testament
> Isaiah 52:7 — “How beautiful on the mountains are the feet of those who bring good news, who proclaim peace...”

## New Testament
> Romans 1:16 — “For I am not ashamed of the gospel, because it is the power of God that brings salvation to everyone who believes...”

## Trivia
- Derived from the Old English 'godspell', meaning 'good story' or 'good tidings'.
- In the Roman Empire, this term was used for imperial announcements, such as the birthday or accession of Caesar.
- The Greek word originally referred to the reward given to a messenger for bringing good news, before coming to mean the message itself.
- While there are four written Gospels, the New Testament often speaks of 'the gospel' as a singular, unified message of salvation.
