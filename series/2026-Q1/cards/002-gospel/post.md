## Word
**GOSPEL** — Good News

## Old Testament
> Isaiah 52:7 — “How beautiful on the mountains are the feet of those who bring good news, who proclaim peace...”

## New Testament
> Romans 1:16 — “For I am not ashamed of the gospel, because it is the power of God that brings salvation to everyone who believes...”

## Trivia
- Derived from Old English 'godspel' (good story), a literal translation of the Greek.
- In the Roman Empire, 'euangelion' was a political term used for the Emperor's birthday or military victories.
- Paul uses the noun form over 60 times, far more than the four Gospels combined.
- The Hebrew equivalent 'besorah' originally referred to a fresh report from a battlefield.
