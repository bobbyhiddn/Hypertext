## Word
**GOSPEL** — Good News

## Old Testament
> Isaiah 52:7 — “How beautiful upon the mountains are the feet of him who brings good news, who publishes peace...”

## New Testament
> Mark 1:15 — “The time is fulfilled, and the kingdom of God is at hand; repent and believe in the gospel.”

## Trivia
- The English word comes from the Old English 'god-spell', meaning 'good story' or 'good tidings'.
- In the Roman Empire, 'euangelion' was a secular term used for announcements of the Emperor's birthday or military victories.
- Mark is the only one of the four evangelists to use the word 'gospel' in the opening line of his book.
- The Hebrew root 'basar' can also mean 'flesh', implying a message that is delivered in person or incarnated.
