## Word
**SCATTER** — To separate and drive off in various directions; to disperse or diffuse.

## Old Testament
> Genesis 11:9 — "The Lord scattered them abroad from there over the face of all the earth."

## New Testament
> Acts 8:4 — "Therefore those who were scattered went everywhere preaching the word."

## Trivia
- The term 'Diaspora' comes from this Greek root, referring to Jews living outside Israel.
- At Babel, scattering was a judgment; in Acts, it became the catalyst for global mission.
- Sheep without a shepherd are frequently described as 'scattered' in scripture.
